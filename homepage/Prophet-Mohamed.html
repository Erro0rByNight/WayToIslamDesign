The Prophet Muhammad

the Prophet Muhammad Peace be Upon Him He is Muhammad bin Abdullah bin Abdul Muttalib bin Hashem bin Abd Manaf, and he was born in the Year of the Elephant on the 12th of Rabi` al-Awwal in the year 53 BC. The Messenger was sent to people to bring them out of darkness into light, and to be an imam, a role model for Muslims and their intercessor on the Day of Resurrection, and he is one of the greatest prophets whom God sent to humanity on earth. Attributes of the Prophet Muhammad Among the qualities and manners that distinguished the Prophet, peace and blessings be upon him, was that he was illiterate, neither writing nor reading despite his eloquence and eloquence in his Arabic language. Honesty, pride, courage, politeness, generosity, asceticism, monotheism, humility, tact, loyalty, friendliness, tenderness in his feelings for others, and obedient to God’s commands. The titles of the Prophet Muhammad The Prophet, peace be upon him, was distinguished by many titles and descriptions that express the appreciation and love of people for him, and the perfection and praise that characterizes him, including: the illiterate, the Messenger of God, Abu al-Qasim, Ahmed, Mahmoud, Mustafa, the Secretary, the Prince, the Conqueror, the martyr, the Rashid, and the Khalil Al-Bashir, Al-Shafi, Al-Da’i, Al-Naji, Al-Manji, Al-Rahim, Al-Hadi, Al-Aziz, Al-Hashimi, Al-Qurashi, Al-Mutahhar, Al-Hijazi, Al-Mansour, Al-Mukhtar, Al-Shafi’, Al-Sadiq, and Al-Karim. About the life of the Prophet Muhammad The Prophet Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him, from Makkah Al-Mukarramah, from the Quraish tribe, and he is an orphan of the father, and his mother is Amina bint Wahb who died at the age of 6, and his breast-feeding Halima Al-Sa’diya, who was raised by his grandfather Abdul Muttalib and when his grandfather died his uncle Abu Talib raised him, and he was working The Prophet Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him, was involved in trade and sheep herding. The Prophet Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him, married Lady Khadija bint Khuwaylid, at the age of 25, and he had his sons, Al-Qasim and Abdullah, and his four daughters, Zainab, Ruqayyah, Umm Kulthum, and Fatimah Al-Zahra. Abu Bakr, Sawda bint Zam’ah, Hafsa bint Omar bin Al-Khattab, Maymoona bint Al-Harith, Umm Habiba, Juweira bint Al-Harith, Zainab bint Jahsh, Safia bint Hayy, Zainab bint Khuzaymah, Umm Salamah, and Maria Al-Qibtiyya, who bore him his son Ibrahim. The revelation was revealed to the Messenger Muhammad, may God’s prayers and peace be upon him, Gabriel while he was sitting worshiping and meditating in the cave of Hira, and he was 40 years old, and he gave him the good tidings of the Islamic call, and he continued to call him and spread Islam in Makkah secretly for 3 years, then the call was announced publicly for 10 years. The Messenger Muhammad, peace be upon him, emigrated from Mecca after 13 years since his mission, due to the severity of the harm he and his polytheistic companions had suffered.
